How Braces Work
By the use of braces, or aligners, we apply different forces to your teeth to make them move in the direction we wish. As we apply the pressure, the bone around the teeth gets remodeled and reformed to the different position we would like the teeth to move to.
Frequently Asked Questions
1.
How do I know if I need braces?
Usually your general dentist can tell you if you need braces due to a problem in your bite, or due to the malalignment of your teeth, and very often you yourself can tell if you need braces or not if you are unhappy with your smile or the crowding of your teeth or any other reason which might make you feel uncomfortable to smile or show your teeth.
2.
Will braces make my teeth perfect?
With braces we definitely can make your teeth straight and your smile beautiful. We always try our best and strive to achieve perfection, however that is not always attainable due to different causes, such as gum disease or getting treatment at a late age, or some other unforseen reasons.
3.
Can you gaurantee the outcome of my treatment?
Orthodontic treatment is just like any other medical treatment. You can never gaurantee that a medical treatment would achieve 100% success, however we always achieve significant improvement on the overwhelming majority of the cases we treat, although we always aim at achieving the best results possible.
4.
What kind of food can I eat when I have braces?
During orthodontic treatment, you should avoid eating anything hard, sticky, or crispy, such as chewing gum, hard or sticky candy, popcorn, corn on the cob, nuts of any kind, or chewing on ice.
5.
Is orthodontic treatment painful?
We move teeth by applying pressure on them. When we first put your braces on, you will not feel except little pressure, however a few hours after you will start feeling some ache which can be resolved by taking some Tylenol or any other over the counter pain medicine. That discomfort should only last for a few days, however you will be back to normal shortly. Usually kids get used to their braces much faster than adults.
6.
Will you accept my insurance?
At our office, we usually accept most insurance coverage, which usually pay a percentage of the fee and you will have to pay the remainder, and of course we will give you a payment plan to fit your budget.
7.
Do I need to wear retainers at the end of treatment?
Yes. Every patient who undergoes orthodontic treatment has to wear retainers to make sure that their teeth do not move back. At our office, retainers are given to the patient at the end of treatment which are included in the price of your treatment.
